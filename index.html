Simultaneous Reveal, Diceless, skirmish game

A miniatures skirmish game where you plan moves and actions on a hidden minimap. It may look awkward from afar, but players attest that it is more intuitive and faster than dial or card based planning. I sought to make a competitive game where the outcome is entirely deterministic from the planning phase, and the only variance is due to the simultaneous play and "input randomness" objectives. I'm no longer a stickler on variance, but it was more important for lengthy (~1 hour) physical games than for quicker mobile games.

Iterative development with public playtests. The design would trend toward simplicity, less components, smaller map, quicker turns.

I sadly shelved this project to refocus on career (2 promotions in 2 years) and digital games to complement. I still love board games!

Custom Engine and Game

Doom and Bloom

Custom 3D​ game engine and networked multiplayer game made in 10 weeks. I contributed all ​3D​ graphics and a lot of gameplay code. Features Splatoon-like gameplay with collaborative texture painting over the network.

  • C++
  • OpenGL
  • GLSL shaders
  • Visual Studio
  • Blender

This was an uninstructed team project in the last quarter of school. I'm proud of this game because it is fun and achieves a certain look, given our time constraint and doing art assets myself. We did a good job of scoping our work for the time available so we were never caught without critical features implemented or with an unstable or unperformant build. Watch the video to see it in action! Features include batched instanced draws and culling, LOD flowers, mocked subsurface scatter, interactive foliage and wind vertex shaders, networked virtual texture painting, first/third person controls.
